I've been going back and forth for about year now.  I've talked about it, researched, and debated it with myself and the one or two people willing to listen to me talk about it.  Should I switch to Linux?

I got my first taste of Linux in college.  I was interested in it then, but I had only used Windows ever since forever.  Windows was all I knew.  It wasn't until a few years later until I made a change...and bought a Mac.

I had seen a guy that I was working with at the time using a Mac, and I was super jealous.  His machine was all metal instead of plastic, it was thin (relatively), and it was light (also relatively).  He was running Windows for the work he was doing in Boot Camp, and I was pretty much sold.  I went out and bought one of my own, which was a 2010 15" Macbook Pro.  

My entire world changed, at least my personal computing world.  It took a couple weeks to really get comfortable with it, but once I did I never looked back.  The interface was modern, beautiful, and it was fast!  I didn't think it could get any better.  I bought one more Macbook Pro in 2013, and an iMac 5K in 2014.  The more time went on, the more frustrated I got with OSX.

I ended up getting a new job where my only software requirement was a machine that can run Citrix Receiver.  I took this opportunity to try to use a Linux machine as my primary workstation, without affecting my day job too much.  

I had a Intel NUC5i7RYH lying around (part of my lab gear) that I hadn't been using very often.  So I wiped it an installed Ubuntu Gnome 16.04.  Everything worked out of the box, including Wi-Fi and Bluetooth.
