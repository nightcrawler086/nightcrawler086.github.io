So, I've switched to Linux...for the most part.

I started out as a Windows user, because that's what was everywhere.  I didn't really know there was anything else out there until college, which was when I really started getting interested in computers.  I learned what open-source software was when I was introduced to Linux and a little about what that meant.  I thought it was the coolest thing I learned about technology to day.

In my current job, I'm lucky enough to work 100% from home.  We use VDI instead of handing out physical machines which gives me a lot of flexibility.  That's actually what gave me the courage to make the switch, because Citrix Receiver runs on pretty much everything.

I've thought about making this switch over the past couple years, but the jobs that I had always had one or two pieces of software that required a Mac or a PC.

This isn't going to be a "I switched to Linux and now everything is perfect" post.  This isn't even a comparison really.  I switched to Linux and changed the way I worked at the same time.

For example, on my Macs I kind of accumulate apps.  Most of the time, they're apps I don't need.  I've tried several different apps for email, text editor apps, GTD (Get Things Done) apps, and more.  I would use them for a couple weeks at most, then forget the exist.  This is something I wanted to stop doing.  Instead of doing a dictation on each this subject, I reduced what I needed out of a computer to a relatively short list:

    - Web Browser
      - For this I settled on [Brave](https://brave.com)
    - Text Editor
      - For this I use GVim/Vim
    - Password Manager
      - [Enpass](https://www.enpass.io)
    - Cloud Storage
      - For this I use a combination of 
        - Amazon Cloud Drive
        - [Odrive Sync Agent](https://docs.odrive.com/docs/odrive-sync-agent)
    - [oh-my-zsh](https://github.com/robbyrussell/oh-my-zsh)
      - because it's better than bash
    - Citrix Receiver

For a list that short and simple, it's hard to justify spending >$2000 on a Macbook Pro or almost $3000 on an iMac.
