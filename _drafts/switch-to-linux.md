I've gone back and forth on writing this several times.  This is something I've considered doing for a couple years now.  The first question to answer is why?

### Why switch to Linux?

There are a couple obvious reasons, but first and foremost is freedom.  The second, and just as important, is security.  Security is becoming such a huge topic today that you really need to take steps as an individual to protect yourself.  

I've been an OSX user for a little over 5 years now.  I made the switch from Windows after I saw some using OSX, and how easy it seemed to be.  So I went out and bought a super-expensive Macbook Pro (15", mid-2010).  It was the first computer that I had used with an SSD in it, which probably made most of the difference.  However, compared to Windows, OSX was like a dream come true.

### OSX (the good parts)

There are a lot of things I like about my Macbook and my other Apple devices.  I currently own a early-2013 Macbook Pro with Retina Display, iPad Mini (first-gen), iMac 5K Retina, two Apple TVs, and an iPhone 6 (ya, I was/is/am kind of a fanboy).  My iPad is really for my daughter now, so I don't really use it anymore.  When I was using it, it was really just for Netflix/Hulu.  Anyways, let's get to the things I like about Apple hardware/software ecosystem:

**iMessage**

This is a pretty big one.  Everyone in my entire family currently owns an Apple smartphone.  There are a few kids in our family, so we all use iMessage to share a lot of photos/videos.  That being said, I don't think I would buy anything other than an iPhone.  It is pretty convenient that all my iMessages hit my laptop too.

**iTunes**

Don't get me wrong here, I'm no lover of the iTunes program.  This is really about the iTunes Store.  If there's an album coming out that I want or a move that I want to buy or rent, I've never had trouble finding it in the iTunes Store.  Any (new) movie that I want to watch is coming from the iTunes Store.  Where this can present a problem is with Music.  I virtually listen to music all day (as long as no one talks to me).
