Studying for this test was different for me than a lot of the other exams that I've studied for.  This one was purely something new that I wanted to learn, without any real relevance to my current job.  All of the other exams I've taken have been directly related to the work I was doing.  This was me stepping out of my comfort zone to try to affect some change in my current career path.

I used the following resources to study for this exam:

- Training videos from [linuxacademy.com](https://linuxacademy.com)
- [AWS Whitepapers](https://aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/)
- [AWS Console](https://aws.amazon.com)

If that resource list looks a little light to you, it's because it is.  This exam was an experiment for me that way.  I wanted to see if I really knew the material.  At the same time I was testing the quality of the training material from [Linux Academy](https://linuxacademy.com).  The short of it is that the material was fantastic.  It was actually better than I thought it was.  Let me explain:

There was over 40 hours of videos for the exam alone.  I went through all of it once, and some of it twice.  I felt confident that I knew what I needed to know for the exam when I sat for it.  However, after the first few questions, I was **much** less comfortable.  I was fairly certain, maybe 10 questions in, that I wasn't going to pass.  That was mostly because I'm used to seeing most of the exam questions before I take the test.

Instead of seeing a question and knowing the answer because I'd seen it before, I had to actually figure them out.  This is why I think the [Linux Academy](https://linuxacademy.com) videos were so good.  I knew enough about the material to be able to answer the questions.  I know that sounds weird (isn't that how all tests go), but it's the first time that's happened to me since I sat for the CCNA exam.  

Here's a few tips for anyone looking to take the AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate exam:

- Security
  - [AWS Security Best Practices](https://s3.amazonaws.com/awsmedia/AWS_Security_Best_Practices.pdf) <- Read this twice
  - Encryption is a big topic.  The above whitepaper will cover it
- High Availability and Fault Tolerance
  - [Building Fault Tolerant Applications on AWS](https://s3.amazonaws.com/awsmedia/AWS_Building_Fault_Tolerant_Applications.pdf) <- Read this twice
- Understand the differences between the "glue" services
  - SQS
  - SWF
  - SMS
- EC2
  - This is a big one. 
  - Understand different instance types
  - Understand when to use the different instance types
    - Reserved Instances
    - Spot Instances
    - On-Demand Instances

Those were the big things on the exam.  Spend as much time in the console as possible.  Do any kind of lab you can find as many times as you can.  If you're looking for an online resource, [Linux Academy](https://linuxacademy.com) is hard to beat.
