Down to what I what to learn and write about.

There are a couple types of jobs that I would be shooting for.  Being a Sysadmin for a decent sized public cloud sounds fun.  I've been a consultant for most of my career, so if that kind of role could marry with these skills that would be cool too.  I'm not really aiming to be any kind of software developer (at least, not right now).  I feel like I would really have to start back at square one to do that.  

I'm a huge advocate for training in general, especially in technical fields like IT.  I get a lot of this from being put in customer-facing situations, without help, that I wasn't trained for.  It eventually made me a better and more agile consultant (and pretty good with Google).  I don't think training can make *anyone* into a great engineer.  I do think that it can build a solid foundation of skills.  Good experience after some proper training will make a great engineer.

That being said, I'm a huge fan of a lot of these online training websites.  My favorite is [Pluralsight](www.pluralsight.com).  They have a really good mix of training aimed at infrastructre engineers and software devlopers, and some things in between.  Also, [Linux Academy](https://linuxacademy.com) has caught my eye recently.  They have a lot of the material I'm interested in right now, and they provide access to 6 real servers to each subscriber for lab/testing.  I don't have a subscription to [Linux Academy](https://linuxacademy.com) right now, but that may change in the near future.

That being said, here's what my current "learning path" consists of.  This list is actually in order, with the idea that the topic builds on the topics previous to it.
