This is going to be my progress report for the month of February.  I've discovered (already) that my initial approach to training in the order I laid out in my [training plan](http://blog.nightcrawler.me/2015/12/30/training-plan/) isn't really going to work.  It can be challenging to stay focused while training when I already understand most of the material.  I've decided to take a different approach.  

Instead of training in a single-threaded sequential way, I'm going to use a more multi-threaded approach.  I'll continue the training in the order I originally laid out, but more as a background process.  I'll be adding a new "foreground" process, which is actually jumping ahead to some of the topics further on in my plan (this is why you see Docker and AWS popping up below).  It helps keep me interested and motivated, and at the same time can shine a light on my weaknesses.  I can use that to generate more (likely smaller) topics to focus on.  Subsequently, this should produce more interesting and focused topics to blog about.

At the same time, I'm also pursuing the  [AWS Certified SysOps Administrator - Associate](https://aws.amazon.com/certification/certified-sysops-admin-associate/) certification.  AWS certifications are some of the most sought after certifications right now, so it's certainly a career move.  I'm intending to fold in through my studies how to interact with AWS programmatically using multiple languages.  This will likely slow my training in terms of number of courses completed.
